Role of the CdV as commodity —

  1. Early utility patent derived from pre-existing technology
  2. Rising Bourgeois class, an emerging market for affordable photography
  3. Small size and low cost created new consumers

Role of the CdV in determining viewer relations —

  • Taken in a photographic studio with props
  • Clothes were offered as costumes so that sitters could present their best — the CdV speaks to their aspirations
  • Physical act of sitting for the photograph is different than previous portraiture, more of the body is visible and posture/mannerisms can indicate class and/or profession
  • Size of the image is decreased, viewing area surrounding the subject increased, mobility of the image as object increased, cheap and quick to proliferate

Role of the CdV in determining social relations —

  • Images were collected — rise of 'celebrity'
  • Some studios sold photos of their customers, i.e. they were collected apart from being accumulated as per custom
  • 'Democracy' of the image

Things to consider —

  • Reproducability (each photograph on the plate was still unique)
  • Disposability of the image and also what type of image, i.e. full body, with background scene, subject to constraints of technology
  • Tradeoffs occur for the mobility of the image in response to market demand (quality, size) — implications
  • Ability to accumulate images, make albums, and 'collect' — the role of the CdV in mediating social relations
